Problem Solving


"Students formulate problem definitions suited for technology-assisted methods such as data analysis, abstract models and algorithmic thinking in exploring and finding solutions."

What I Do:

Practice problem solving daily, whether it be troubleshooting a bug on my computer, fixing broken code, or analyzing patterns in software.

Utlilize Data


"Students collect data or identify relevant data sets, use digital tools to analyze them, and represent data in various ways to facilitate problem-solving and decision-making."

What I do:

Utilize surveys and other means of online data-collection and draw conclusions through visual analysis. (ex: A google form on student's tech literacy, excel sheet sorting results, and charts communicating trends in results)

Breaking down + Modeling


"Students break problems into component parts, extract key information, and develop descriptive models to understand complex systems or facilitate problem-solving."

What I Do:

When encountering a problem, I determine the root of the problem before seeking solutions. By understanding the fundementals, the solution process is made easier.

Automation

"Students understand how automation works and use algorithmic thinking to develop a sequence of steps to create and test automated solutions."

What I do:

Using technology and computers to complete tedious tasks (such as calculating or sorting data). This can entail developing my own programs with code to complete a task.


What you can do:

1.Think of a problem you've faced with technology / wait for one to arise

Try to troubleshoot this problem through guessing and checking before the solution you'd typically reach for (asking for help / looking it up)

Skill: Problem solving

2. Learn to code

Go to codeacademy.com or any other introductory programming website to learn the basics of programming

Skill: Automation and Problem Solving

3. Use a self-made or pre-existing program to replace something you'd otherwise do by hand

Consider using a google form to track peer responses or develop a program to calculate the grade you need on your final rather than working out the equation yourself.

ex: RogerHub, Ben Eggleston
Skill: Automation and data collection
